PANDA

The giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) is a bear species native to the mountain ranges of central China. It is known for its distinctive black-and-white fur and love of bamboo. Adult pandas can weigh up to 160 kg (350 lb) and are solitary animals that spend most of their time feeding and resting.

Though classified as carnivores, pandas are almost entirely herbivorous, with bamboo making up over 99% of their diet. They have a specially adapted wrist bone that acts like a thumb to grasp bamboo stalks. Pandas are gentle and shy, rarely aggressive unless provoked.

Once on the brink of extinction, panda populations have rebounded due to dedicated conservation efforts, although they remain vulnerable.
