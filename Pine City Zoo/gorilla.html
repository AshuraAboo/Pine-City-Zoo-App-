GORILLA

Gorillas are the largest living primates and belong to the genus Gorilla. Native to the forests of central Sub-Saharan Africa, they are divided into two species—the eastern gorilla and the western gorilla—with several subspecies. Gorillas are ground-dwelling, predominantly herbivorous, and live in social groups called troops led by a dominant silverback male.

They are known for their intelligence, strength, and gentle nature. Adult males can weigh up to 180 kg (400 lb) and reach a height of 1.75 m (5 ft 9 in) when standing. Gorillas communicate using vocalizations, body postures, and facial expressions. They build nests for sleeping and forage for fruits, leaves, and stems during the day.

Despite their strength, gorillas are generally shy and avoid confrontation. Their populations are threatened by habitat loss, disease, and poaching.
